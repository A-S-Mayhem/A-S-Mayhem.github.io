The following document lists datasets on several topics. I have tried to
compile different datasets that can be useful for political science
students. If the links are not working, email me! Also, **Please do not
share without permission of the author!**

# General statistics

-   **ICPSR**: <https://www.icpsr.umich.edu/icpsrweb/>\
    According to their website: "ICPSR maintains a data archive of more than 250,000 files of research in the social and behavioral sciences.
    It hosts 21 specialized collections of data in education, aging, criminal justice, substance abuse, terrorism, and other fields".

-   **World Bank**: <https://data.worldbank.org/>\
    DataBank is an analysis and visualisation tool that contains collections of time series data on a variety of topics.

-   **PENN World Table**: <https://www.rug.nl/ggdc/productivity/pwt/>\
    PWT version 10.0 is a database with information on relative levels of income, output, input and productivity, covering 183 countries between 1950 and 2019.

-   **Cross-National Time-Series (CNTS) Data Archive**: <https://www.cntsdata.com/the-data>\
    More than 200 years of annual Excel data from 1815 onward for over 200 countries, including countries and empires that no longer exist.
    Warning: requires purchasing!

-   **IMF**: <https://www.imf.org/en/Data>\
    The IMF publishes a range of time series data on IMF lending, exchange rates and other economic and financial indicators. Manuals,
    guides, and other material on statistical practices at the IMF, in member countries, and of the statistical community at large are also
    available.

-   **United Nations Human Development**: <http://hdr.undp.org/>

-   **World Values Survey (WVS)**: <http://www.worldvaluessurvey.org/wvs.jsp>\
    The survey, which started in 1981, seeks to use the most rigorous, high-quality research designs in each country. The WVS consists of
    nationally representative surveys conducted in almost 100 countries which contain almost 90 percent of the world's population, using a
    common questionnaire.

-   **Database for Political Institutions by World Bank**: <https://datacatalog.worldbank.org/dataset/wps2283-database-political-institutions>

# Measurements of democracy and political regimes

-   **[xmarquez](https://github.com/xmarquez)** compiled a mega GitHub list: <https://xmarquez.github.io/democracyData/>

-   **Freedom House**: <https://freedomhouse.org/report/freedom-world>\
    Freedom House's report is the first and best source available on the state of political and civil rights around the world, equally useful
    to scholars and practitioners interested in the state of democracy and human freedom.

-   **Polity IV**: <http://www.systemicpeace.org/polityproject.html>\
    The Polity IV dataset covers all major, independent states in the global system over the period 1800-2017 (i.e., states with a total
    population of 500,000 or more in the most recent year; currently 167 countries).

-   **Democracy-Dictatorship Index**: <https://sites.google.com/site/joseantoniocheibub/datasets/democracy-and-dictatorship-revisited>\
    Classification of political regimes as democracy and dictatorship. Classification of democracies as parliamentary, semi-presidential
    (mixed) and presidential. Classification of dictatorships as military, civilian and royal. Coverage: 202 countries, from 1946 or
    year of independence to 2008.

-   **Svolik's Data (2012)**: <https://campuspress.yale.edu/svolik/the-politics-of-authoritarian-rule/>\
    From the "Politics of Authoritarian Rule\". Contains three different data: Regime Data, Institutions Data, Leader and Ruling Coalition Data.

-   **Varieties of Democracy (V-Dem)**: <https://www.v-dem.net/en/>\
    The V-Dem project distinguishes between five high-level principles of democracy: electoral, liberal, participatory, deliberative, and
    egalitarian, and collects data to measure these principles.

-   **Autocratic Regime Data**: <https://sites.psu.edu/dictators/>\
    Project by Geddes, Wright, and Frantz. Similar to Svolik's data, but extensive in terms of year coverage.

-   **Authoritarian Regimes Dataset**: <https://sites.google.com/site/authoritarianregimedataset/>\
    Project by Wahman, Teorell, and Hadenius - comprehensive dataset on authoritarian regimes in the world between 1972-2017.

-   **Global State of Democracy Indices (GSoD Indices**: <https://www.idea.int/gsod-indices/about>\
    The GSoD Indices are based on 116 individual indicators devised by various scholars and organizations using different types of sources:
    expert surveys, standards-based coding by research groups and analysts, observational data and composite measures.

-   **Comparative Political Data Set**: <https://www.cpds-data.org/>\
    It consists of annual data for 36 democratic countries for the period of 1960 to 2020 or since their transition to democracy.

-   **Presidential Power**: <https://presidential-power.net/?page_id=2148>\
    The Presidential Power Blog was established in September 2013 on the initiative Professor Robert Elgie, bringing together a range of
    scholars interested in presidents and presidential politics.

-   **Semipresidentialism**: <http://www.semipresidentialism.com/>\
    Semi-presidentialism is where there is a popularly elected fixed-term president and a prime minister and cabinet who are
    collectively responsible to the legislature.

-   **Democracy Tools**: <https://www.andresduribe.com/democracytools/>\
    This project, entitled "Developing a Global Early-Warning System for Democratic Erosion," has led to the creation of three publicly
    accessible tools to allow all interested parties to study, track, and predict democratic erosion in countries around the world. The
    tools in the system cover three areas of democratic functioning: 1) tracking global democratic performance, 2) analyzing the rhetorical
    strategies of politicians, and 3) understanding the role of referendums in healthy democracies.

-   **The Episodes of Regime Transformation (ERT) dataset**: <https://v-dem.net/data/ert-dataset/>\
    Identifies episodes of democratization (liberalizing autocracy, democratic deepening) and autocratization (democratic regression,
    autocratic regression) in the most recent V-Dem dataset.

-   **Rulers, Elections and Irregular Governance (REIGN)**: <https://oefdatascience.github.io/REIGN.github.io/>

-   **National Elections Across Democracy and Autocracy (NELDA)**: <https://nelda.co/>

-   **Party Facts**: <https://partyfacts.herokuapp.com/data/>\
    This dataset has links to [all datasets and articles related to political parties](https://partyfacts.herokuapp.com/data/).

-   **IDEA**: <https://www.idea.int/data-tools>

-   **Political Parties, Presidents, Elections, and Governments (PPEG)**: <https://ppeg.wzb.eu/>

-   **Global Elections Database**: <http://www.globalelectionsdatabase.com/index.php/index>

-   **Electoral Legacies of War: Political Competition in Postwar Southeast Europe**: <https://elwar.uni.lu/>

-   **Archigos - A Data Base on Leaders 1875 - 2004**: <https://www.rochester.edu/college/faculty/hgoemans/data.htm>

-   **Political Representation, Executives, and Political Parties Survey (PREPPS)**: <http://wiesehomeier.net/prepps/ppla/>\
    Policy positions of 146 political parties and 18 presidents in 18 Latin American countries. The survey measured policy positions on
    general dimensions such as economic or social policy, but also on specific dimensions such as minority rights or security questions.
    Countries covered are Argentina, Bolivia, Brazil, Chile, Colombia, Costa Rica, Ecuador, El Salvador, Guatemala, Honduras, Mexico,
    Nicaragua, Panama, Paraguay, Peru, the Dominican Republic, Uruguay and Venezuela.

-   **Chapel Hill Expert Survey - CHES Data**: <https://www.chesdata.eu/our-surveys>\
    The first survey was conducted in 1999, with subsequent waves in 2002, 2006, 2010, 2014, and 2019. The number of countries increased
    from 14 Western European countries in 1999 to 24 current or prospective EU members in 2006 to 32 countries in 2019. In this
    time, the number of national parties grew from 143 to 277. The 2019 survey includes all EU member states, plus parties in Iceland,
    Norway, Switzerland, and Turkey.

-   **Democratic Accountability and Linkages Project (DALP)**:     <https://sites.duke.edu/democracylinkage/>\
    Covers 21 countries in the Americas, 13 countries in the Asia-Pacific region, 18 countries in post-communist Eastern and
    Central Europe, 16 countries in Western Europe, and 20 countries in Africa and the Middle East for a total of 88 countries. The first
    wave of 2008-2009 expert surveys (DALP I) involved data collection in more than 80 countries.

-   **V-Party by V-Dem**: <https://www.v-dem.net/vpartyds.html>

-   **Global Party Survey by Norris (2019)**: <https://www.globalpartysurvey.org/>\
    The Global Party Survey compares the ideology, policy positions, and rhetoric of political parties around the world. The first survey
    dataset, GPS-2019, draws upon estimates from 1861 experts and covers 1043 political parties in 163 countries.

-   **World Statesmen**: <https://www.worldstatesmen.org/>

-   **Comparative Constitutions Project**: <http://comparativeconstitutionsproject.org/>

-   **Constitute Project**: <https://constituteproject.org/?lang=en>

-   **Matt Golder's Elections**: <http://mattgolder.com/elections>

-   **Electoral System Change in Europe since 1945**: <https://www.electoralsystemchanges.eu/Public/DatasPage.php?ID=6>

-   **Political Party Database**: <https://www.politicalpartydb.org/about/>

-   **Voter Turnout Database**: <https://www.idea.int/data-tools/data/voter-turnout>
