--- title: "Work in Progress: Comparing methods of Mentorship Matching" excerpt: "Mentorship programs are a great way for young new employees to learn from their more experienced colleagues, but it is also a great way for more seasoned employees to widen their perspective. The compatability of mentors/mentees is therefore an important part of the program design." collection: portfolioecon --- Back to the Research/Coding Portfolio

In this project I use python to explore various methods of matching mentors to mentees, assuming we are given a spreadsheet containing the compatability measure between the mentor/mentee pairs. (I leave the project of determining an appropriate method of scoring compatability for another day). My current method is to minimize loss of not getting your top preference. Work is still needed to be done on alternative methods and how to compare them. The method of measuring success also needs to be defined. Do I look at overall satisfaction/compatability or should I instead look at the average? Should I look at the range? There are a lot of questions still surrounding this work, but that is why it is interesting.
