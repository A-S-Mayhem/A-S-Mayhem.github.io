--- layout: archive title: "Teaching" permalink: /teaching/ author_profile: true --- The courses I have designed and taught at the undergraduate and graduate levels can be grouped into the following three large areas:
  1. cognitive science, philosophy of mind, and philosophy of (cognitive) science;
  2. history of philosophy;
  3. environmental ethics & enviromental philosophy.
Reflecting my interests and orientation as a researcher, in my teaching I like to emphasize the intersection of questions in these distinct areas. I have done this, for instance, by emphasizing the "environmental" and "ecological" dimensions of mind (area #1, connecting to #3), focusing on changing views on mind and science in my historical courses (area #2, connecting to #1), and discussing how environmental issues relate to conceptions of what humans are like and to perspectives on development in science and technology (area #3, connecting to #1 and #2). {% include base_path %} {% for post in site.teaching reversed %} {% include archive-single.html %} {% endfor %}
