As a climate economist with a background in climate science, Dr. Lisa Thalheimer has diverse experience in multi-disciplinary quantitative research and scientific products.  She is currently a <a href='https://ehs.unu.edu/about/departments/migration-and-environment#team' target="_blank">Senior Researcher</a> at the <a href='https://ehs.unu.edu/' target="_blank">United Nations University - Institute for Environment and Human Security </a> (UNU-EHS) in Bonn, Germany.  Her research uses statistical methods to attribute extreme weather events and human-induced climate change, and human responses such as migration, displacement and conflict.  For updates on Lisa's research interests and publications click <a href='https://lisathalheimer.github.io/publications/' target="_blank">here</a>. 

Lisa completed a postdoctoral fellowshiop at Princeton University's Center for Policy Research on Energy and the Environment (<a href='https://cpree.princeton.edu/people/lisa-thalheimer' target="_blank">C-PREE</a>) with Prof. Michael Oppenheimer in 2022 and her PhD at the Environmental Change Institute at the University of Oxford with Prof. Fredi Otto.  She graduated from Columbia University with a MS in Sustainability Management.
